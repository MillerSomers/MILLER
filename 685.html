John CONDUITT, Esq. 1

  • Born: Mar 1688, Hampshire County, England
  • Christened: Mar 8, 1688, London, England
  • Marriage: Catherine BARTON in 1717
  • Died: May 23, 1737 at age 49
  • Buried: Westminster Abbey, London, England

  General Notes:

OCCUPATION: Succeeded Sir Isaac Newton as England's "Master of the Royal Mint" after Newton's death
NOTEWORTHY: John was educated at Westminster School and Cambridge University;
Member of Parliament for Whitchurch, Hampshire;
Recorded biographical material about Sir Isaac Newton;
Commissioned the Westminster Abbey Tomb of Sir Isaac Newton;
In 1711 he was Judge Advocate in Portugal with the British forces and was a Captain of Dragoons;
Sir Isaac Newton took up residence towards the end of his life at the home of John & Catherine Conduitt in Cranbury Park, near Winchester
BURIAL: A black & white marble monument dedicated to John Conduitt is located at Westminster Abbey
