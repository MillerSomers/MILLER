  General Notes:

OCCUPATION: Farmer (1880 U.S. Census)
RESIDENCES: Marion County, AR (1880 U.S. Census);
Campbell, Lawrence County, AR (1900 U.S. Census)

MILITARY: Per Forest Greeley Wilson --
Civil War, Army of Confederate States, enlisted June 9, 1861 at Fort Smith, Arkansas. He traveled from Walnut Ridge, Lawrence County, Arkansas, a distance of 240 miles to enlist. He was paid $100.00 for his horse and $15.00 for his equipment. His pay was 40 cents a day. He fought in the following battles:

Nesho, Missouri, July 5, 1861
Wilson's Creek, August 10, 1861
Pea Ridge, March 7 - 8, 1862
Richmond, Kentucky, August 29-30, 1862
Murfreesboro, December 3l, 1862 - January 3, 1863
Jackson siege, July, 1863
Chickamauga, September 19-20, 1863
Chicamauga Siege, September - November, 1863
Atlanta Campaign, May - September, 1864
Dug Gap, May 8, 1864
Resaca, May 14-15, 1864
Peach Tree Creek, July 20, 1864
Atlanta, July 22, 1864
Ezra Church, July 28, 1864
Atlanta Siege, July - September, 1864
Jonesboro, August 31 - September 1, 1864
Lovejoy's Station, September 2-5, 1864
Moon's Station, October 4, 1864
Franklin, November 30, 1864
Nashville, December 15-16, 1864
Sugar Creek, December 26, 1864
Carolinas Campaign, February - April, 1865
Bentonville, March 19-21, 1865

Pvt. Sylvester Wilson, along with his unit, surrendered to the Union forces on April 26, 1865, at Durham Station, North Carolina.
