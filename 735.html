Joseph Hoyle MENDENHALL 1 2

  • Born: Oct 30, 1811, Gaston County, North Carolina
  • Marriage: Abigail Rebecca RUDISILL on May 27, 1834 in Lincoln County, North Carolina
  • Died: Nov 23, 1865, Jenny Lind, Sebastian County, Arkansas at age 54

  General Notes:

ANCESTRY: Descended from Warinus de la Strode (born 1020 in Bretagne, France), a French knight who lived at the time of William the Conqueror
ANCESTRY: 6th Great-Grandchild of John Mildenhall Jr. (born 1560), the only Englishmen to visit both royal courts of the Emperors Akbar and Jahangir, and the first Englishmen to be buried in India
ANCESTRY: Great-Nephew of John Bartram, the "Father of American Botany"
ANCESTRY: First-Cousin (Twice Removed) to William Bartram, "America's first Ornithologist"
ANCESTRY: Second-Cousin (Once Removed) to Thomas Corwin Mendenhall (born 1841), Alaska's Mendenhall Glacier's namesake

NOTEWORTHY: Joseph moved to Arkansas from North Carolina in the early 1840's along with his brother, Jacob. He first settled in the Russellville area with Jacob, but about 1850 moved to Carroll County. Later, Jacob moved to the Fort Smith area and Joseph went with him.
Joseph was a farmer. He ran for local office and in 1858 and in 1860 ran unsuccessfully for the state legislature.
When the Civil War broke out in April of 1861, a citizen's group met to consider what course they should pursue in connection with the war. Joseph was appointed to a committee to draft resolutions expressive of the sense of the meeting. After due deliberation the committee reported as follows:
Whereas, The administration of Lincoln, with a spirit of duplicity equaled only by its total disregard of every patriotic duty, has inaugurated a policy of coercion and subjugation by force of arms, arraying brothers against brothers: therefore be it, resolved, by the citizens of Sebastian County, of all parties, in mass assembled, that we will resist such policy on the part of the administration even to the death and to this end we pledge our lives, our fortunes and our sacred honor.
